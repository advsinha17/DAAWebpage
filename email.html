Dataset Description

The Enron email network dataset is an email communication dataset with around half a million emails. Nodes of the network are email addresses and if an address i sent at least one email to address j, the graph contains an undirected edge from i to j. This dataset has 36,692 nodes and 183,831 edges. Distribution of Cliques and Runtime Comparison

All 3 algorithms were run on the email enron dataset. The network has a total of 226,859 maximal cliques and the size of the largest maximal clique is 20 vertices.

The graph on the left above contains the runtimes of each algorithm for the dataset. The Bron-Kerbosch and CLIQUE (Tomita) algorithm both take only 1.13 seconds and 1.94 seconds respectively, while the Chiba algorithm takes 458 seconds, due to the greater time complexity of the algorithm. The graph on the right shows the distribution of the maximal cliques of different sizes. The graph peaks at a maximal clique size of 7, with 25.896k cliques having size 7.
